SWE Spring 2021 Outreach Programs - Overview

Sign up here to receive updates on our programs!


üëã #HelloWorld

#HelloWorld is a program that introduces middle school girls to the basics of web design and programming (including HTML, CSS, and Python). They will create and design their own website, learning to integrate and present new ideas and information in a web-based format, as well as get support from MIT SWE mentors. This semester, #HelloWorld will be held virtually over Zoom.


üìö College Connection

The College Connection initiative hosts an e-mentoring program for female high school students looking to connect with inspiring MIT students who can provide insight into the college application process, answer any college-related questions, and give advice. Mentees are given the opportunity to meet weekly in small groups with their mentors and to attend social activities where they can mingle with other groups.

The SWE Engineering Activity Kits program is an initiative of MIT Society of Women Engineers to continue increasing interest in STEM through the distribution of free science and engineering kits to students of grade levels 3rd-8th, especially for those from lower income backgrounds. We hope to reach students across the nation and empower them to engage in science and technology, especially during these virtual times. We will be additionally sending out a digital SWE Science magazine with compiled experiments and fun, hands-on activities students can perform easily at home.


üé° Festival

Festival aims to bring MIT SWE to science festivals and other large-scale events. This year, we are participating in the Cambridge Science Festival! More information can be found here: https://cambridgesciencefestival.org/.


üî≠ Outreach Seminar Series

Seminar Series is an initiative that plans seminar-style events for elementary through high school students. Topics include different majors, STEM fields, career options, and research areas, as well as the college application process. Panelists include MIT students, faculty, and industry professionals.


üß™ STEAM Saturdays

STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, Mathematics) Saturday is an experimental, hands-on initiative for elementary students (specifically for students who attend Prospect Hill Academy). The STEAM board members will develop four hour-long different curriculums per semester that will encapsulate different fields of STEAM. Past examples of presentations/ experiments include making spaghetti bridges, paper origami, lava lamp, and catapult.


üìñ SWE-Course

SWE-Course is a new online course that will allow students to continue learning fun STEM content virtually! The topic for the course this semester will be centered around space exploration and rocket engineering. This course plans to run 3 sessions over 3 consecutive weeks on April 24, May 1, and May 8 at 3pm Eastern. Content will be crafted for middle-school age students in mind, but students outside of this age range may also register if interested. Although the course will be held virtually, we are hoping to send build kits to all registered students, so that everyone can participate in a fun hands-on activity with the class! Use this form (here) to register interested students. We will cap the class at 25 students, but the first 15 students will receive free kits (not necessary for the course)! For any questions regarding this program, contact Sabrina Liu (sabrliu@mit.edu).

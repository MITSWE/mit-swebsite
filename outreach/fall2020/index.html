The Engineering Activity Kits Program aims to reach students with less digital resources through engineering activity kits - complete with instructions, explanations and materials - mailed directly to students!


üëã #HelloWorld

#HelloWorld is a program that teaches middle school girls the basics of web design (including HTML, CSS, and Javascript), as well as to explore new, exciting STEM topics in an independent manner, using multimedia resources from other websites, online journals, videos, and books, and help from MIT student mentors. They will study a subject of their choosing and design a website to reflect what they've researched, learning to integrate and present this new information in a web-based format. This semester, #HelloWorld will be held virtually over Zoom.


üèÖ SWE Challenges

SWE Challenges is a series of design-build challenges and problem solving activities for students to complete at home. Students will be able to submit their completed challenges/activities for prizes, raffles, and more!


üìñ SWE-Course

SWE-Course is a new online course that will allow students to continue learning fun STEM content virtually! The topic for the course this semester will be centered around the science of climate change and the environment. This course plans to run 3 sessions over 3 consecutive weeks in November (Nov. 7, Nov. 14, Nov. 21). Content will be crafted for middle-school age students in mind. For any questions regarding this program, contact Nicole Cybul (ncybul@mit.edu).

The SWE Outreach Seminar Series will hold seminars and panels for students addressing topics like careers, different research areas in STEM, majors, applying to college, etc. Potential speakers and panelists will include MIT students and clubs, professors, and industry professionals.


üìö College Connection

College Connection is an e-mentorship program that pairs high school females with MIT students, providing mentees with college advising and general advice.


üß™ STEAM Saturdays

STEAM Saturdays are going virtual! This program runs weekly at the end of the semester on October 24 and 31 and November 7 and 14. The chairs work with Prospect Hill Academy, a local Boston school, to teach hour-long classes to elementary school students to get them involved in STEAM in a fun and practical way.

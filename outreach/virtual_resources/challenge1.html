SWE Challenge 1: Build an Emergency Shelter!


Recommended age: 7 - 11 years old

Goal: Build a shelter to fit your favorite stuffed animal / toy!

Estimated time: 45 minutes

Prize Deadline: Fill out our survey by October 26, 2020 to be entered in a raffle for some fun prizes!

  • Plastic bottles/water bottles

Breaking news! In the aftermath of a hurricane, thousands of people in Massachusetts need emergency shelters, and they need them fast.

Q: What is an emergency shelter? Who might need one, and why?

Engineers and inventors help rebuild lives by inventing and designing temporary housing that can be built quickly, easily, and cheaply, so that people can have a safe (and warm!) place to live.

How can you make sure your design conserves energy by keeping heat in when it's cold outside?

What shapes are the strongest? Watch this video for a quick demonstration of the strength of a rectangle vs arch vs triangle.

Q: How can you apply this information to your design?


üìç Brainstorm and Design (10 minutes)

Prototyping should be done quickly. Test the prototype and, if it doesn't work, redesign (maybe several times).

  • What is important for a good emergency shelter?
  • What would be speedy to build? Remember, this is an emergency!
  • How will you make a sturdy frame?
  • What will you cover your shelter with? Will it keep your toy warm at night?
  • How will your toy get in and out of your shelter?
